Criminogenic Factors — Environmental Conditions
Environmental conditions influence when and where crimes occur. This sub-module analyzes temporal patterns, situational exposure, and spatial concentration of crime. By examining time-based risk windows, crime type interactions, and geographic clustering, it identifies environments that facilitate criminal opportunities. These insights support preventive patrol planning.

High-Risk Time Windows (Time Heatmap)

This heatmap visualizes crime intensity across hours and days. Darker regions indicate peak crime periods. Identifying these windows allows law enforcement to adjust patrol schedules dynamically. It reflects opportunity-based criminology theories.

Gender × Crime Type Interaction (Stacked Bar)

This stacked bar chart shows how different crime types affect genders differently. It reveals interaction between environment and victimization. Such insights help design gender-sensitive safety measures and targeted interventions in specific crime domains.

Spatial Environmental Risk (Interactive Map)

The interactive map displays geographic crime concentration. Areas with dense markers indicate environmental risk hotspots. Spatial visualization complements temporal analysis by revealing location-specific exposure. This helps prioritize region-based preventive strategies.
