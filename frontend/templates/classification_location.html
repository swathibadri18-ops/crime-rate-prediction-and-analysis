ML Classification — Location-Based Crime Prediction
Location-Based Crime Prediction estimates regional crime vulnerability using probabilistic risk modeling. It identifies high-risk zones and categorizes cities into risk levels for strategic deployment planning.

Risk Score by Area

This chart presents the predicted crime risk score for each city based on machine learning probability outputs. The risk score is calculated on a percentage scale, indicating the likelihood of high-risk classification. Higher scores suggest elevated crime vulnerability in specific regions. This visualization enables comparative analysis across multiple cities. It supports strategic patrol allocation and helps law enforcement prioritize areas requiring intensified monitoring and preventive measures.

Risk Distribution — Crime Vulnerability Segmentation

The risk distribution chart categorizes cities into high, medium, and low-risk groups based on probability thresholds. A higher concentration of high-risk zones may indicate systemic structural issues requiring policy intervention. Balanced distribution suggests stable regional crime dynamics. This classification framework supports strategic planning and enhances decision-making by translating model predictions into actionable categories.
