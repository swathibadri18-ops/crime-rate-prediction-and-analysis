Criminogenic Factors — Socio-economic Indicators
Socio-economic indicators explain how demographic and structural conditions influence crime patterns. This sub-module analyzes victim vulnerability, policing intensity, and overall crime pressure. By examining age distribution, variable relationships, and combined risk profiles, it highlights social environments where crime is more likely to emerge.

Victim Age Vulnerability (Box Plot)

This box plot visualizes the distribution of victim ages, highlighting median values, spread, and outliers. Concentration within specific age ranges indicates vulnerable population groups. Outliers reveal extreme cases involving minors or elderly victims, supporting targeted social protection strategies.

Correlation Heatmap (Variable Relationships)

This heatmap shows the strength of relationships between victim age and police deployment. Strong correlations indicate structural dependencies, while weaker relationships suggest independent influences. Such analysis helps distinguish causal patterns from coincidental trends.

Socio-economic Risk Profile (Radar Chart)

The radar chart aggregates socio-economic indicators into a single risk profile. It compares age vulnerability, policing presence, and crime pressure simultaneously. This holistic view supports strategic planning and high-level policy decisions.
