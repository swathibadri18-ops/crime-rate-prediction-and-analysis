Geographic Distribution of Crime

Geographic analysis visualizes how crime incidents are spread across cities. It helps identify persistent urban hotspots, reveals spatial crime patterns, and supports location-specific intervention strategies. This analysis directly influences patrol planning and hotspot policing.

Crime Count by City

This visualization compares crime volumes across major cities. Cities with consistently higher crime counts are identified as persistent hotspots. Such regions require focused surveillance and targeted law enforcement strategies. The comparison helps prioritize resource allocation. It also supports urban safety planning.

Crime Domain Distribution by City

This chart highlights how different crime types dominate specific cities. Understanding city-specific crime profiles allows tailored intervention strategies. It supports crime-type focused policing. This insight improves predictive feature engineering. It strengthens crime prevention planning.

Hotspot Concentration Insight

Cities showing high crime density across multiple categories represent long-term hotspots. These locations are critical for predictive policing. Continuous monitoring enables proactive intervention. Such insights reduce crime escalation. They form the basis for risk forecasting models.
