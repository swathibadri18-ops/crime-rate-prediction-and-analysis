Temporal Crime Trends

Temporal Trends Analysis examines how crime patterns change over time. By analyzing yearly, monthly, and weekly distributions, this module identifies high-risk periods and recurring temporal patterns. These insights help anticipate future incidents and support proactive policing strategies. Temporal features also play a critical role in crime prediction models.

Year-wise Crime Trend

This visualization illustrates how the total number of crime incidents has evolved over the years. Long-term upward or downward trends help evaluate the effectiveness of policing policies and interventions. Sudden increases may indicate emerging threats or socio-economic changes. Declining trends often reflect successful crime prevention strategies.

Monthly Crime Distribution

This chart highlights seasonal variations in crime occurrence. Certain months often experience higher crime rates due to social events, holidays, or environmental factors. Understanding monthly patterns enables law enforcement agencies to prepare for seasonal crime spikes. These insights significantly improve short-term crime forecasting accuracy.

Crime by Day of Week

Crime frequency varies significantly across different days of the week. This analysis identifies high-risk days such as weekends or specific weekdays. Such insights support optimized police deployment and patrol scheduling. Day-level trends also reflect behavioral and social activity patterns relevant for predictive modeling.
